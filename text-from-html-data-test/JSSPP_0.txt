  JSSPP 2025  
 28 th  Workshop on Job Scheduling Strategies for Parallel Processing (JSSPP 2025)  
  In Conjunction with IPDPS 2025, Milan, Italy, June 3, 2025  
   
 Welcome to JSSPP 2025 Workshop  
   
   JSSPP 2025 is the 28 th  edition of the workshop on Job Scheduling Strategies for Parallel Processing  . JSSPP 2025 will be held in conjunction with 39th IEEE International Parallel and Distributed Processing Symposium (IPDPS'2025), June 3 - 7, 2025 in Milan, Italy.  
   
 This annual series of workshops has been held since 1995 in conjunction with various other conferences, including IPDPS  (formerly IPPS), the main parallel processing symposium sponsored by the IEEE, the ACM SIGMETRICS  and ICS  conferences, and HPDC  / GGF  , the premier annual meeting of the Global Grid Forum.  
 The proceedings of these workshops are published by Springer-Verlag in the Lecture Notes in Computer Science Series  .  
  You can download BibTeX file of all these workshop proceedings  .  
   
 JSSPP 2025 is planned as in-person meeting  .  Submission is open  .

 News:   
   JSSPP 2025 will be part of IPDPS 2025 in Milan, Italy   
 We are happy to announce that the JSSPP 2025 will be held in conjunction with 39th IEEE International Parallel and Distributed Processing Symposium (IPDPS'2025)  , June 3 - 7, 2025 in Milan, Italy. JSSPP 2024 proceedings to appear as Springer's LNCS 14591   
 We are happy to announce that the proceedings of JSSPP 2024 - 27th Workshop on Job Scheduling Strategies for Parallel Processing  will be published in LNCS series by Springer. Keynote Lecture is Online   
 JSSPP Keynote was delivered by Walfredo Cirne (Google): Managing Private Clouds   .  
  Abstract: This talk defines the Private Cloud Management program and presents Flex, Google’s solution for it. It covers how Flex makes Google’s easier to operate, as well as the key techniques used to make it more efficient. It also discusses how we preserve governance by charging internal users the resources they prompted Google to buy, in spite of aggressive resource sharing and on-demand allocation.
