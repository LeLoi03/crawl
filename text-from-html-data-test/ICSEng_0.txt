 Invitation  

 Anticipate the 31st edition of the International Conference on Systems Engineering  

 Scheduled for October 22-24, 2024, in Warsaw, Poland.  

 Since 1974, this conference has been serving as a gathering point for researchers, practitioners, and experts in systems engineering, providing a platform for the exchange of cutting-edge ideas and fostering global collaboration particularly on the topics of machine learning, artificial intelligence, big data, intelligent systems, IoT, cybersecurity in networks and safety engineering.  
 This year, for the first time in Warsaw, hosted by NASK – National Research Institute and Military University of Technology, we invite contributions that delve into diverse aspects of systems and safety engineering, spanning areas such as technology integration, applications, process optimization, and innovative methodologies. Join us in shaping the discourse on the latest advancements and challenges in the dynamic field of systems engineering.

 ON THE AGENDA   

 Scope of the conference  

 The Conference will focus on Systems Engineering, highlighting applications across various domains. Sessions will encompass:  

 Artificial Intelligence & Cyber Technologies:  

 machine learning 
  AI applications in medicine and biometrics 
  cybersecurity across various systems 
  large language models 

 Computing & Networks:  

 cloud computing 
  smart networks 
  distributed computing 
  data center management 
  Internet of Things 

 Advanced Sciences & Engineering:  

 advanced mathematics 
  computer science 
  systems engineering practices 
  control systems 
  computer simulations 

 Data Science & Analytics:  

 Big Data 
  social network analysis 
  information and communication systems 

 Innovation in Systems:  

 industrial automation 
  robotics 
  environmental systems 
  Covid19 solutions 

 Specialized Systems:  

 hardware systems 
  bioengineering 
  biosurveillance 
  aerospace systems 
  power systems 

 Emerging Technologies:  

 quantum computing 
  cryptology 
  propagation of (dis)information 

 Applications & Society:  

 gaming and entertainment systems 
  medical diagnostic systems 
  ethical aspects of technology
