  <h2 class="h h2">
          Aims &amp; Scope
      </h2>


  
    
                  <p><em>Intelligent Data Analysis</em> provides a forum for the examination of issues related to the research and applications of Artificial Intelligence techniques in data analysis across a variety of disciplines. These techniques include (but are not limited to): all areas of data visualization, data pre-processing (fusion, editing, transformation, filtering, sampling), data engineering, database mining techniques, tools and applications, use of domain knowledge in data analysis, big data applications, evolutionary algorithms, machine learning, neural nets, fuzzy logic, statistical pattern recognition, knowledge filtering, and post-processing. In particular, papers are preferred that discuss the development of new AI-related data analysis architectures, methodologies, and techniques and their applications to various domains.</p>
<p>Papers published in this journal are geared heavily towards applications, with an anticipated split of 70% of the papers published being applications-oriented, research and the remaining 30% containing more theoretical research. Manuscripts should be submitted in PDF format only. Please prepare your manuscripts in single-space, and include figures and tables in the body of the text where they are referred to. For all enquiries regarding the submission of your manuscript please contact the IDA journal editor: <a href="mailto:editor1@ida-ij.com">editor1@ida-ij.com</a></p>

  <h2 class="h h2">
          Author Guidelines
      </h2>


  
    
                  <p><strong>SUBMISSION OF MANUSCRIPT</strong></p>
<p>By submitting my article to this journal, I agree to the&nbsp;<a href="http://iospress.com/author-copyright-agreement" target="_blank" title="‌" rel="noreferrer ugc nofollow">Author Copyright Agreement</a>, the&nbsp;<a href="http://iospress.com/ethics-policy" target="_blank" title="‌" rel="noreferrer ugc nofollow">IOS Press Ethics Policy</a>, and the&nbsp;<a href="http://iospress.com/privacy-policy" target="_blank" title="‌" rel="noreferrer ugc nofollow">IOS Press Privacy Policy</a>.</p>
<p>Authors are requested to submit their manuscript electronically to <a href="https://www.editorialmanager.com/ida/default2.aspx" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the journal's editorial management&nbsp;system</a>.</p>
<p>Authors are encouraged to use the <a href="https://www.iospress.nl/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/texsupport.iospress-ida-master.zip">IDA LaTeX style file</a> and to follow the formatting rules explained in more detail below. MsWord users can prepare their paper as an unformatted double-spaced manuscript. When an article is accepted for publication the publisher will ensure that the article is typeset according to the journal style.</p>
<p><em>Intelligent Data Analysis</em> invites the submission of research and application articles that comply with the aims and scope of the journal. In particular, articles that discuss development of new AI architectures, methodologies, and techniques and their applications to the field of data analysis are preferred. Manuscripts should be submitted in *.pdf format only. Please prepare your manuscripts in single-space, and include figures and tables in the body of the text where they are referred to. Manuscripts are received with the understanding that their content is unpublished material and is not being submitted for publication elsewhere. Further, it is understood that each co-author has made substantial contributions to the work described and that each accepts joint responsibility for publication.</p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB" xml:lang="EN-GB"><strong>Open access option</strong></span><br><span lang="EN-US" xml:lang="EN-US">The </span><a href="http://www.iospress.nl/service/authors/open-library-ios-press-open-access-option/" target="_blank"><span>IOS Press Open Library</span></a><span lang="EN-US" xml:lang="EN-US">&nbsp;offers authors an open access option. By selecting the open access option, the article will be freely available from the moment it is published, also in the pre-press module. In the Open Library&nbsp;the article processing charges are paid in the form of an open access fee. Authors will receive an order form upon acceptance of their article. Open access is entirely optional. &nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB" xml:lang="EN-GB"><strong>Publication types and fees</strong></span></p>
<p><span lang="EN-GB" xml:lang="EN-GB">When an article is accepted for publication, authors are required to pay a publication fee that depends on the publication type:</span></p>
<ul><li><span lang="EN-GB" xml:lang="EN-GB">Subscription-based publication (restricted access): </span>US$450 / €450<span lang="EN-GB" xml:lang="EN-GB">.</span></li>
<li><span lang="EN-GB" xml:lang="EN-GB">Open Access option (CC BY-NC 4.0): </span>US$1500 / €1500<span lang="EN-GB" xml:lang="EN-GB">.</span></li>
<li><span lang="EN-GB" xml:lang="EN-GB">Open Access option without the Non-Commercial clause (CC BY 4.0): </span>US$2150/€2150.</li>
</ul><p><span lang="EN-GB" xml:lang="EN-GB">Page charges do not apply to feature articles.</span></p>
<p>Waivers are available for corresponding authors from low-income countries as identified by the Hinari Project. A waiver request for the publication fee should be submitted before the initial submission of an article.</p>
<p><strong>Required files</strong><br>After the article has been accepted, the authors should submit the final version as source files, including a word processor file of the text, such as Word or LateX (If using LaTeX, please use the standard article.sty as a style file and also send a PDF version of the LaTeX file).</p>
<p><strong>Colour figures</strong><br>It is possible to have figures printed in colour, provided the cost of their reproduction is paid for by the author. See Preparation of Manuscripts for the required file formats.</p>
<p><strong>PREPARATION OF MANUSCRIPTS</strong></p>
<p><strong>Organization of the paper and style of presentation</strong><br>Manuscripts must be written in English. Authors whose native language is not English are advised to consult a professional English language editing service or a native English speaker prior to submission.</p>
<p>Manuscripts should be prepared with wide margins and double (single) spacing throughout, including the abstract, footnotes and references. Manuscripts should be submitted in *.pdf format only. Every page of the manuscript, including the title page, references, tables, etc., should be numbered. However, in the text no reference should be made to page numbers; if necessary, one may refer to sections. Try to avoid the excessive use of italics and bold face.</p>
<p>Manuscripts should be organized in the following order:</p>
<p>Title page</p>
<p>Body of text (divided by subheadings) + Tables, Figures and Figure captions</p>
<p>Acknowledgements</p>
<p>References</p>
<p>Headings and subheadings should be numbered and typed on a separate line, without indentation.</p>
<p>SI units should be used, i.e., the units based on the metre, kilogramme, second, etc.</p>
<p><strong>Title page</strong><br>The title page should provide the following information:</p>
<p>Title (should be clear, descriptive and not too long)</p>
<p>Name(s) of author(s); please indicate who is the corresponding author</p>
<p>Full affiliation(s)</p>
<p>Present address of author(s), if different from affiliation</p>
<p>Complete address of corresponding author, including tel. no., fax no. and e-mail address</p>
<p>Abstract; should be clear, descriptive, self-explanatory and not longer than 200 words, it should also be suitable for publication in abstracting services</p>
<p>Keywords</p>
<p><strong>Tables</strong></p>
<p>Figures and Tables</p>
<p>Number as Table 1, Table 2 etc, and refer to all of them in the text.</p>
<p>Figures and Tables should be included in the text in the exact location where they are referred to.</p>
<p>Each table should have a brief and self-explanatory title.</p>
<p>Column headings should be brief, but sufficiently explanatory. Standard abbreviations of units of measurement should be added between parentheses.</p>
<p>Vertical lines should not be used to separate columns. Leave some extra space between the columns instead.</p>
<p>Any explanations essential to the understanding of the table should be given in footnotes at the bottom of the table.</p>
<p><strong>REFERENCES</strong></p>
<p>Place citations as numbers in square brackets in the text. All publications cited in the text should be presented in an alphabetical list of references at the end of the manuscript in the following style:</p>
<p>[1] B. Newman and E.T. Liu, Perspective on BRCA1, <em>Breast Disease</em> <strong>10</strong> (1998), 3-10.<br>[2] D.F. Pilkey, Happy conservation laws, in: <em>Neural Stresses</em>, J. Frost, ed., Controlled Press, Georgia, 1995, pp. 332-391.<br>[3] E. Wilson, <em>Active vibration analysis of thin-walled beam</em>s, Ph.D. Dissertation, University of Virginia, 1991.</p>
<p><strong>Footnotes</strong><br>Footnotes should only be used if absolutely essential. In most cases it is possible to incorporate the information in the text.</p>
<ul><li>If used, they should be numbered in the text, indicated by superscript numbers and kept as short as possible.</li>
</ul><p><strong>Figures</strong></p>
<p>Number figures as Fig. 1, Fig. 2, etc and refer to all of them in the text.</p>
<p>Each figure should be provided on a separate sheet. Figures should not be included in the text.</p>
<p>Colour figures can be included, provided the cost of their reproduction is paid for by the author.</p>
<p>For the file formats of the figures please take the following into account:</p>
<p>Line art should be have a minimum resolution of 600 dpi, save as EPS or TIFF</p>
<p>Grayscales (incl photos) should have a minimum resolution of 300 dpi (no lettering), or 500 dpi (when there is lettering); save as tiff</p>
<p>Do not save figures as JPEG, this format may lose information in the process</p>
<p>Do not use figures taken from the Internet, the resolution will be too low for printing</p>
<p>Do not use colour in your figures if they are to be printed in black &amp; white, as this will reduce the print quality (note that in software often the default is colour, you should change the settings)</p>
<p>For figures that should be printed in colour, please send a CMYK encoded EPS or TIFF</p>
<p>Figures should be designed with the format of the page of the journal in mind. They should be of such a size as to allow a reduction of 50%.</p>
<p>On maps and other figures where a scale is needed, use bar scales rather than numerical ones, i.e., do not use scales of the type 1:10,000. This avoids problems if the figures need to be reduced.</p>
<p>Each figure should have a self-explanatory caption. The captions to all figures should be typed on a separate sheet of the manuscript.</p>
<p>Photographs are only acceptable if they have good contrast and intensity.</p>

                  <h2>Open Access</h2>
<p dir="auto">By default, articles published in&nbsp;<em>Intelligent Data Analysis</em>&nbsp;are available only to institutions and individuals with access rights. However, the journal offers all authors the option to purchase open access publication for their article as part of the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.iospress.com/open-library" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer ugc nofollow">IOS Press Open Library</a>. This means that the final published version will be freely available to anyone worldwide, indefinitely, under a Creative Commons license and without the need to purchase access to the article. This is also referred to as “gold” open access.</p>
<p dir="auto"><strong>Gold open access pricing</strong><br>Authors who choose gold open access publication will be subject to an article publication charge of €1500 / US$1500 for publication under the&nbsp;<a href="https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc/4.0/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer ugc nofollow">CC BY-NC 4.0</a>&nbsp;license or €2150 / US$2150 for publication under the&nbsp;<a href="https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer ugc nofollow">CC BY 4.0</a>&nbsp;license. Pricing is exclusive of possible taxes. After an article is accepted for publication, the corresponding author will be informed regarding the open access option during the production stages, and will have the opportunity to purchase open access for their article. It could be that the open access fee of an article is waived completely due an institutional agreement IOS Press has with the corresponding authors' institution. Please check the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.iospress.com/open-access-institutional-agreements" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer ugc nofollow">institutional agreements</a>&nbsp;page for details.</p>
<p dir="auto"><strong>Green open access</strong><br>Authors who do not make use of the gold open access option may still make their article freely available using self-archiving, also referred to as green open access. Authors may make their final accepted manuscript available for free download from their personal or institutional website or institutional archive. This model is free for the author.</p>
