            <div property="schema:text" class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-body field--type-text-with-summary field--label-hidden field__item"><p style="text-align:justify; margin-bottom:11px">We invite workshop proposals for the 2023 IEEE Winter Conference of Applications on Computer Vision (WACV 2023). WACV 2023 is planned as an in-person conference with associated in-person workshops in Waikoloa, Hawaii, January 3-7, 2023. However, proposals for purely virtual workshops will also be considered.</p>

<p>The workshop proposals should focus on topics related to computer vision and its applications, interdisciplinary themes with other scientific and application areas, as well as emerging challenges or competitions. The format, style, and content of accepted workshops are under the control of the workshop organizers and largely autonomous from the main conference. Workshop organizers are expected to manage their technical programs, invite experts in the domain, and maintain a website for the workshop. Workshop registration and logistics will be handled by the main conference.</p>

<p>We encourage workshop proposals aimed at creating or strengthening communities.</p>

<p>Accepted workshops will be held on either 3 January 2023 or 7 January 2023. The workshop paper submission, review, acceptance notification, and final manuscript submission should all be handled between 10 October 2022 (tentative deadline for submission of workshop papers) and 19 November 2022 (firm camera-ready workshop paper due date).</p>

<p>Conference website: <a href="https://wacv2023.thecvf.com/">https://wacv2023.thecvf.com/</a> &nbsp;</p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p><strong>Submission Instructions</strong></p>

<p>Proposals should clearly specify/discuss the following:</p>

<ul><li>Workshop title, topic and tentative call for papers</li>
	<li>Motivation, impact, and expected outcomes</li>
	<li>Brief bios of organizers (including email and web pages)</li>
	<li>Tentative program committee</li>
	<li>Format (select either half or full day) and preliminary schedule</li>
	<li>Type of workshop, i.e., in-person or virtual (the latter requires a short justification)</li>
	<li>Tentative invited speakers</li>
	<li>Estimated number of submissions and attendees</li>
	<li>Plans (if any) for a special journal issue or book</li>
	<li>Social impact of the workshop</li>
</ul><p>&nbsp;</p>

<p>Workshop proposals should be submitted in the form of a single PDF through CMT: <a href="https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/WACVworkshops2023">https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/WACVworkshops2023</a>.</p>

<p>Proposals will be evaluated by the workshop chairs and conference organizers, with an eye towards selecting high-quality workshops on a diverse set of topics that will inform and inspire the community.</p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p><strong>Diversity, equity and inclusion</strong></p>

<p>We especially encourage proposals involving diverse organizing teams and communities currently under-represented at WACV. We also welcome workshop topics that address issues in Diversity, Equity and Inclusion in Computer Vision applications.</p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p><strong>Contact and Queries</strong></p>

<p>For questions and concerns, please contact</p>

<p>Adam Czajka (<a href="mailto:aczajka@nd.edu">aczajka@nd.edu</a>) and Vitomir&nbsp;Å truc (<a href="mailto:vitomir.struc@fe.uni-lj.si">vitomir.struc@fe.uni-lj.si</a>)&nbsp;</p>

<p>Workshops Co-Chairs WACV 2023</p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p><strong>Important Dates</strong></p>

<p>Workshop proposal deadline: August 8, 2022</p>

<p>Workshop acceptance notification: August 15, 2022</p>

<p>Workshop dates: 3 January or 7 January 2023</p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p>Submission</p>
