        <div class="intro">
            <div class="article__byline">
                <span class="article__byline_author">By 
            <div>Gail Pieper</div>
      </span><span class="divider">|</span><span class="article__byline_publicationdate">
            <div><time datetime="2023-11-01T12:00:00Z">November 1, 2023</time>
</div>
      </span>
            </div>
                        <div class="lede">
                    
            <div>Hui Zhou, a principal software development specialist in the <span class="caps">MCS</span> division at Argonne, was co-author of <span class="push-double"></span>​<span class="pull-double">“</span><span class="caps">MPI</span> Application Binary Interface Standardization,” which received the Best Paper award at the EuroMPI <span class="push-single"></span>​<span class="pull-single">’</span>23 conference in September 2023.</div>
      
            </div>
                    </div>

                    <figure class="featured-image wysiwyg_align-right">
                <img src="https://www.anl.gov/sites/www/files/styles/article_teaser_16x9/public/eurompi23-cover-16x9.jpg?h=8abcec71&amp;itok=ySqlHqpO" alt="Featured article image">
                <figcaption class="image-caption"></figcaption>
            </figure>
        
        <div class="article-page__content">
            
            <div><div class="tex2jax_process"><p><span class="caps">MPI</span>, or the Message Passing Interface, is widely used for high-performance computing workloads. While it is standardized in terms of the C and Fortran programming languages, however, it is not implementation agnostic; and the increasing adoption of <span class="caps">MPI</span> by applications written in languages other than C and Fortran has motivated the development of an application binary interface (<span class="caps">ABI</span>).</p>
<p>The proposal presented in this award-winning paper represents recent efforts by the <span class="caps">MPI</span> Forum’s <span class="caps">ABI</span> working group to develop a standard <span class="caps">ABI</span> for <span class="caps">MPI</span> that effectively addresses this issue.</p>
<p><span class="dquo">“</span>We successfully tested our prototype implementation both within <span class="caps">MPICH</span> and as an independent abstraction layer,” Zhou said.</p>
<p>The researchers acknowledge that realizing the full potential of a standard <span class="caps">ABI</span> will likely take some time. The <span class="caps">ABI</span> must be standardized by the <span class="caps">MPI</span> Forum and implemented by the major implementations such as <span class="caps">MPICH</span>; and <span class="caps">MPI</span> users will have to recompile everything against the <span class="caps">MPI</span> <span class="caps">ABI</span>.&nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>Nevertheless, Zhou and his colleagues say that they are confident that ultimately having a standard <span class="caps">MPI</span> <span class="caps">ABI</span> will <span class="push-double"></span>​<span class="pull-double">“</span>significantly reduce the pain of using <span class="caps">MPI</span> in a variety of contexts and will encourage greater use of <span class="caps">MPI</span> in new domains.”</p>
<p>For the full paper, see J. Hammond, L. D, Dalcin, Erik Schnetter, M. PéRache, J.-B. Besnard, J. Brown, G. B. Gadeschi, S. Byrne, J. Schuchart, and H. Zhou, <span class="push-double"></span>​<span class="pull-double">“</span><a href="https://dl.acm.org/doi/fullHtml/10.1145/3615318.3615319#:~:text=MPI%20is%20the%20most%20widely,new%20architectures%20for%20many%20years" target="_blank"><span class="caps">MPI</span> Application Binary Interface Standardization</a>,” in EuroMPI <span class="push-single"></span>​<span class="pull-single">’</span>23: Proceedings of the 30<sup class="ordinal">th</sup> European <span class="caps">MPI</span> Users’ Group Meeting, September 2023, Article No.: 1, Pages 1–12.</p>
</div></div>
