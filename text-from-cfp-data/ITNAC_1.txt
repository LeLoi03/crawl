<a href="/sites/default/files/2024/keynote2024-ma.pdf" target="_blank">Read more</a></p></td></tr></tbody></table><h2>ITNAC Focus Topics</h2><div class="row right__focus_topics"><div class="col-sm-7"><div class="focus__"><p>With the increasing focus on modelling and simulation in the fields of cyber-networks, data mining, cyber security, distributed computing, mobile computing, cognitive computing, cloud computing, computing tools, applications, simulation tools, system performance and data and computer communications the demand for high quality research outcomes has never been greater. ITNAC has been the forum for researchers and engineers to present and discuss topics related to advanced computing and data communication network technologies, services and applications.</p><p>Novel contributions are presented in the form of keynote speeches by international experts, peer-reviewed technical papers, and posters. ITNAC 2024&nbsp;seeks to address and capture highly innovative and state-of-the-art research from academia, industry and standardization bodies.</p><p>ITNAC 2024&nbsp;is an international conference and forum for the presentation of research outcomes covering timely and relevant aspects concerning modelling and simulation of distributed, mobile, cognitive and cloud computing, computer and data communications, local and metropolitan networks, optical, wired and wireless telecommunication networks and applications. Contributions are welcome on all advanced research and particularly (but not limited to) on the following topics:</p></div></div><div class="col-sm-5"><div class="topics__"><ul><li>Wireline networks</li><li>Wireless networks</li><li><span>Modelling and Simulation</span></li><li><span>Cyber-Networks, Data Mining and Cyber-Security</span></li><li><span>Distributed, Cognitive and Cloud Computing</span></li><li><span>Network&nbsp;Applications &amp; Convergence</span></li><li><span>Local and Metropolitan Networks</span></li><li><span>Community and corporate Wi-Fi</span></li><li><span>Data Communications</span></li><li><span>Networks and Management</span></li><li><span>Software Defined Networking</span></li><li><span>Internet Technologies and Applications</span></li><li>Emerging Technologies</li><li><span>Mobility and Vehicular Networks</span></li><li><span>Mobile Cellular and Wireless Networks</span></li><li><span>Optical Communications</span></li><li><span>Wireless Sensor Networks</span></li><li><span>Power Efficiency and Sustainability</span></li></ul></div></div></div><div class="row"><div class="col-sm-7"><div class="focus__"><h3>Wireline Networks</h3><p>The field of wireline networking has evolved in recent years and the shift to fibre networks is increasing. Advances in optical communications, hybrid networks, and the utilization of new and innovative approaches to make networks more reliable and resilient has continued.</p><p>The goal of this track is to provide a forum for the presentation of new advances, ideas, and solutions from theoretical, experimental, and applied research to address specific new challenges and emerging issues concerned with this field. We seek papers describing original, previously unpublished research results.</p></div></div><div class="col-sm-5"><div class="topics__"><p>Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:</p><ul><li>Optical Communications &amp; Networking</li><li>Cloud &amp; Data Center Networks</li><li>Software-Defined Infrastructure</li><li>(Networks, Storage, and Compute)</li><li>Core Network Architectures and Protocols</li><li>Network Resilience</li><li>Anomaly Detection</li><li>Reliability Performance Analysis</li><li>Carrier Networks</li><li>Metro, Local &amp; Access Networks</li><li>Submarine Networks</li><li>High-performance Routing</li><li>Heterogeneous Networks</li><li>Network Architectures for Smart Grid</li><li>Hybrid Wireline-Wireless Networks</li><li>xDSL networks</li></ul></div></div></div><div class="row right__focus_topics"><div class="col-sm-7"><div class="focus__"><h3>Mobile &amp; Wireless Networks</h3><p>The field of mobile and wireless networks is a rapidly evolving area. Recent advances in technologies for emerging mobile and wireless networks, including mobile ad hoc networks, vehicular networks, 5G/6G cellular networks, among others, have the potential to enable many new mobile and wireless services and applications that can profoundly impact our lives in positive ways.</p><p>The goal of this track is to provide a forum for the presentation of new advances, ideas, and solutions from theoretical, experimental, and applied research to address specific new challenges and emerging issues concerned with this field. We seek papers describing original, previously unpublished research results.</p></div></div><div class="col-sm-5"><div class="topics__"><p>Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:</p><ul><li>5G and 6G</li><li>Wireless access and routing protocols</li><li>Cross-layer design optimisation</li><li>Green, low power, energy efficient and sustainable networking</li><li>Nature and bio-inspired approaches to networking</li><li>Network-based mobile positioning and tracking systems</li><li>Cognitive and cooperative principles for networking</li><li>Inter-working, integration, and convergence issues</li><li>Mobile social and ambient networks</li><li>Mobile and fixed wireless broadband access networks</li><li>QoS provisioning and resource management</li><li>Terminal and network mobility</li><li>Traffic engineering, congestion and admission control</li><li>Techno-economic analysis and business models for emerging networks</li><li>Novel network-enabled applications and services</li><li>Networking standards and regulations</li><li>Security and privacy issues</li></ul></div></div></div><div class="row"><div class="col-sm-7"><div class="focus__"><h3>Internet and Communications Technologies</h3><p>In the current and the envisioned future Internet, a variety of new technologies and applications is emerging. New networking architectures and design concepts are to be developed which consider interactions with the real world, as well as emerging issues like energy-efficiency or socio-economic aspects. A holistic view is necessary which takes into account the network of the Future, the Internet of services, media and enterprise Internet, but also the Internet of Things. However, there are still significant challenges for the theoretical understanding and on the deployment of Internet technologies.</p><p>The goal of the Internet technology track is to bring together people from academia and industry and to stimulate discussions on future Internet applications and future wireline and wireless Internet architectures to accelerate their development. We seek papers describing original, previously unpublished research results.</p><p><img src="/sites/default/files/inline-images/tech_img.jpg" data-entity-uuid="dc3b2de7-0560-4aed-819a-bdd6c059af98" data-entity-type="file" alt="tech" width="380" height="275" loading="lazy"></p></div></div><div class="col-sm-5"><div class="topics__"><p>Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:</p><h6>Emerging Technologies:</h6><ul><li>Virtualization technology and programmability of FI elements</li><li>Service-oriented architectures and service composition</li><li>Future Internet routing schemes or transport concepts</li><li>IPv6 and its derivatives</li><li>Economic Traffic Management</li><li>Massive MIMO</li><li>Mesh networks, ad-hoc networks, sensor networks, femtocells</li><li>Self-configuring and self-optimizing cellular networks</li><li>Dynamic Spectrum Access and Cognitive Radio</li><li>Airborne Communications</li><li>Ultra-reliable and Low-Latency Communications</li><li>Hybrid Satellite-Terrestrial Communications</li><li>Nano and Molecular Communications and Networks</li><li>Vehicular Networks</li></ul><h6>Emerging Applications:</h6><ul><li>Content-centric networks</li><li>Social networks</li><li>Multilevel and location-aware mobile services</li><li>Cloud computing</li><li>Software as a service</li><li>Grid computing</li><li>Peer-to-peer networks and overlays</li><li>Multimedia support e.g. vehicle-2-X communications</li><li>Internet governance</li></ul><h6>Emerging Issues:</h6><ul><li>Artificial Intelligence</li><li>Next Generation Internet Architecture</li><li>Energy efficiency and energy awareness</li><li>Network application awareness</li><li>Network management systems and control plane</li><li>Coarse-grained QoS solutions for scalable Future Internet services</li><li>Quality of Experience</li><li>Support of mobility of devices, users, sessions, networks, and services</li><li>Security and privacy mechanism</li><li>Flexibility to realize new innovations</li></ul></div></div></div><div class="row right__focus_topics"><div class="col-sm-7"><div class="focus__"><img src="/sites/default/files/inline-images/type_koo.jpg" data-entity-uuid="f2c2dc86-66c7-4602-9d08-7461631dd28e" data-entity-type="file" alt="rde" width="382" height="350" loading="lazy"></div></div><div class="col-sm-5"><div class="topics__"><h3>IPv6 mobility and vehicular networks</h3><ul><li>Mobility management and topology control</li><li>Location-based services and positioning</li><li>Micro and macro-mobility</li><li>Mobility, location and handoff management</li><li>Mobile and wireless IPv6</li><li>IPv6 security</li><li>Wireless broadband mobile access</li><li>ad hoc and sensor networks</li><li>Wireless multicasting</li><li>Wireless mesh networks</li><li>Topology control in wireless networks</li><li>Physical and MAC layer issues</li><li>IPv6 GeoNetworking</li><li>Cross-layer design and optimization for vehicular networks and cognitive networks</li><li>Security issues for vehicular and cognitive networks</li></ul></div></div></div><div class="row"><div class="col-sm-7"><div class="focus__"><h3>Network Applications and Convergence</h3><p>Network-Based Applications are a fast growing area for network services.</p><p>The goal of this track is to provide a forum for the presentation of new advances, ideas, and solutions from theoretical, experimental, and applied research to address specific new challenges and emerging issues concerned with this field. We seek papers describing original, previously unpublished research results.</p></div></div><div class="col-sm-5"><div class="topics__"><p>Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:</p><ul><li>Software Defined Networks (SDN)</li><li>SDN as Enabling Technology for 5G</li><li>Network Virtualization</li><li>Cloud &amp; Crowd Computing</li><li>Smart Homes &amp; Smart Cities &amp; Smart Buildings</li><li>Resource Planning &amp; Management</li><li>Smartphone Networked Apps</li><li>Content Delivery Networks</li><li>Big Data &amp; Analytics</li><li>Augmented &amp; Virtual Reality</li><li>Cyber-Physical Systems &amp; Networks</li><li>Game Theory in Networks</li><li>Tactile Internet</li><li>Access Control</li><li>Scheduling Algorithms</li><li>Security, Trust and Privacy</li></ul></div></div></div><div class="row right__focus_topics"><div class="col-sm-7"><div class="focus__"><h3>Optical Communications</h3><p>Optical communication technologies will continue to be increasingly important in supporting the future Internet's expected scaling requirements of billions of users, their IT needs and aggregated huge bandwidths. Over the last two decades, optical communication technologies have increased the transmission capacity per fiber by several orders of magnitude, achieving Tbit/s transmissions. If the data traffic usage continues to increase at the current rate, a further increase in the transmission capacity of several orders of magnitude will be needed over the coming decades. This implies that future optical systems and networks should be able to support capacities well over Peta bit/s. However, the current technologies have already begun to reveal several fundamental limits; the electronic speed limit, the Shannon and quantum limit, and the IP<span>&nbsp;</span>bottleneck. The future technologies must overcome these limits ensuring sustainable growth of network traffic. The optical communications theme aims to provide a forum for the presentation and discussion of significant progress of research, development and applications of cutting-edge technologies in optical communication devices, subsystems, systems and networks.</p><img src="/sites/default/files/inline-images/Optical%20Communications.jpg" data-entity-uuid="8463be77-db46-4943-9222-8be4aba3c579" data-entity-type="file" alt="ttt" width="400" height="390" loading="lazy"></div></div><div class="col-sm-5"><div class="topics__"><p>Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:</p><p>Next Generation Broadband Access Networks, Subsystems and Systems</p><ul><li>Optical Ethernet, EPON/GPON, 10Gb/s Ethernet, 100Gb/s Ethernet</li><li>WDM Access Networks, WDM-PON</li><li>Wired/wireless convergence, Telecom/broadcast convergence, IPTV</li><li>Hybrid optical-wireless access networks, Radio-over-fiber</li><li>Higher order modulations and OFDM in optical access networks</li><li>Fiber-to-the-Home/Fiber-to-the-Curve (FTTH/FTTC)</li><li>Grid/cloud computing over optical networks</li><li>Green Internet and energy efficiency</li></ul><p>Next Generation Optical Networks, Subsystems and Systems</p><ul><li>Optical core network architecture, design, control, and management</li><li>Optical cross-connect/add-drop multiplexers, ROADM, and switching subsystems</li><li>Optical packet/burst/flow switching networks and subsystems</li><li>Large capacity optical transmission, WDM, OTDM</li><li>OFDM, higher order modulations and advanced modulation formats in photonics</li><li>Energy efficient optical networks</li><li>Impairments mitigations and performance monitoring techniques</li><li>Digital signal processing in photonics systems</li><li>Free Space optical communications</li></ul><p>Optical Fiber, Components, and Devices</p><ul><li>Fiber design, characterization, fabrication, installation,, and maintenance</li><li>Photonics Crystal fibers</li><li>Polymer/non-silica fibers</li><li>Optical active device and modules</li><li>Optical passive device and modules</li><li>Fiber Bragg grating, fiber lasers/amplifiers, MUX/DEMUX, and demodulators</li><li>Silicon photonics</li><li>Optical MEM</li></ul></div></div></div><div class="row"><div class="col-sm-7"><div class="focus__"><h3>Wireless Sensor Networks</h3><p>The field of wireless sensor networks is now getting more and more mature, but new design concepts, experimental and theoretical findings, and applications continue to emerge at a rapid pace. Furthermore, there are still significant challenges for the theoretical understanding and practical application of sensor networks.</p><p>The goal of the sensor networking track is to bring together people from academia and industry who have interest in the area of wireless ad hoc and sensor networks. We seek papers describing original, previously unpublished research results.</p></div></div><div class="col-sm-5"><div class="topics__"><p>Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:</p><ul><li>Networking protocols: MAC, routing, transport, next generation</li><li>Cooperative communication approaches</li><li>Cross-layer design and optimization</li><li>Broadcasting, multicasting, geocasting</li><li>Quality-of-service, reliability and fault tolerance, coverage and connectivity</li><li>Security</li><li>Supplementary services: localization, time synchronization</li><li>Body sensor networks</li><li>Operating Systems and Software</li><li>Middleware and Macroprogramming</li><li>Information and query processing</li><li>Prototypes, field experiments, testbeds</li><li>Theoretical limits, network scaling</li><li>Novel applications</li></ul></div></div></div><div class="row right__focus_topics"><div class="col-sm-7"><div class="focus__"><h3>Networks and Management</h3><p>Telecommunications and networking policy, regulation and management are an important aspect of deregulation and competition policy formulation. There are significant challenges for the development of an understanding of the dynamics of telecommunications markets. The&nbsp;introduction of solutions and case studies provide an opportunity to analyse outcomes and to learn from research into policy and management practices.</p></div></div><div class="col-sm-5"><div class="topics__"><p>Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:</p><ul><li>General topics related to networks and applications</li><li>Broadband Network Management</li><li>Regional and Remote Networks</li><li>Next generation network regulation</li><li>Communication technology fundamentals</li></ul></div></div></div></div></div></div></div>
